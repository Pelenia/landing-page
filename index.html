Why Decentralize Mass Media?

A long time ago, "entertainment" was shared by all. In the past century, Big Tube has convinced us that "content is king" and must be regulated by middlemen. Even in the internet generation, voices are silenced, content is lost, and creators have to participate in advertising and data collection in order to make a living. New tools have arisen to enable creators to engage their patrons, but these platforms continue to glorify the content itself instead of the creation process.

We believe in the power of the people—we can make our own entertainment, use smart contracts to organize it, and blockchains to get paid. Unlike the centralized media conglomerates that create all of our movies, series, and news, PELE distributes value to content creators and their benefactors, instead of rent-seekers like Big Tube.

You saw it here first--welcome to DeMe (decentralized media).

What's Cryptocurrency?

A blockchain is a tamper-proof ledger whose entries and history are all verified throughout a decentralized network of participants around the world. A cryptocurrency, meanwhile, is the original application for blockchain technology. It is money that can't be faked!

A cryptocurrency is not simply digital money, but a means of transacting without the influence of a centralized authority (such as a central bank, which has the ability to inflate a nation's currency at will). Cryptocurrencies are most often used in conjunction with decentralized projects or endeavors, but this does not always mean a cryptocurrency is decentralized.

While many crypto projects have good intentions, it has proven extremely difficult to truly decentralize a network at its early stages. Even Bitcoin's early life could have been considered centralized by its anonymous founder, Satoshi Nakamoto. Satoshi walked away from Bitcoin roughly two years after he introduced it to the world, when he presumably considered the project decentralized enough to keep itself alive.

What's a Smart Contract?

A smart contract is a tool to eliminate the middle man from transactions, interactions, or other *-actions* relevant to its purpose. It generally runs on top of a blockchain. The cryptocurrency Ethereum was one of the first blockchain projects to explicitly support smart contracts. Zilliqa, our "host" blockchain, is another generation of blockchains designed for smart contract operations.

From Wikipedia: "A smart contract is a computer program or a transaction protocol which is intended to automatically execute, control or document legally relevant events and actions according to the terms of a contract or an agreement. The objectives of smart contracts are the reduction of need in trusted intermediators, arbitrations and enforcement costs, fraud losses, as well as the reduction of malicious and accidental exceptions."

PFTs

The Project Fungible Token (PFT) is PELE's standardized method of tokenizing media projects, a temporary store of value quantifying public interest in a project. Creators may opt to initially fund an escrow to prove their commitment to a new project, increasing confidence for buyers of PFTs by locking in a sum of $PELE to be released at the completion of the creative work, successful or not. PFTs can be allocated by their creators to creative contributors, sold to patrons, and/or distributed to project participants to form a DAO. PFTs can be exchanged between users, redeemed for a variety of possible interactions such as privileged experiences, bidding on NFTs and/or, upon completion, melted into either its corresponding share of the project's escrow or a unique badge that verifies the holder's contribution.


With a fixed 100 PFTs per project pool, the scarce nature of the PFT incentivizes holders to participate constructively with the project's creator even if their intentions are purely speculative. Since a single PFT represents a 1% stake in the project, PFT ownership provides proportional access to a project's escrow, voting rights (in the case of a DAO), and proportional influence when a badge is minted for the holder's profile. While the $PELE token provides holders access to the network's tools, PFTs allow for intelligent participation within communities.

What is Big Tube?

Despite the mass media's usage of terms like "Big Oil," "Big Pharma," or "Big Tobacco," Big Tube has neglected to lump itself in the same category of larger-than-life conglomerates with its own social and political agendas. Big Tube is centralized mass media.

For almost a century, Big Tube has slowly distorted the very essence of entertainment by turning content into a commodity and industrializing its creation. Traditional activities like dance or campfire anthems that once fulfilled the human desire for recreation in a community setting have been appropriated by rent-seeking gate keepers stifling the natural impulse for creativity, diversion and social interactions by distracting viewers with pretty faces, new technologies, and elite marketing budgets. Until very recently, most humans were unable to easily share thoughts, stories or information across large swathes of space and time. As this has become possible using online platforms, Big Tube has continued to set its own rules for engagement, both communicated and unspoken, frequently disguised as protocol-level rules built-in to its functionality such as algorithmic search results that corral audiences into conforming. The primary purpose of contemporary content platforms is to encourage the consumption of more content, which is a proxy for Big Tube's real goal: to fill the pockets of shareholders and further consolidate their influence.
